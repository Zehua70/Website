SP has done many things to help in environment sustainability

Singapore Poly launches country's first-ever solar photovoltaics recycling line

  • Solar panel recycling machine: The system involves a machine to take apart solar panels, so its components - including precious materials like silver and glass - can be recycled.
  • Target capacity: It has a target recycling capacity of 80 panels a day and a recovery rate of up to 95%.
  • Further establishes solar research labs for innovation: The Polytechnic has also established two laboratories - SP-Sembcorp Solar PV Research Lab and SP-CHINT Smart Electrical Power Training Laboratory to enhance research and development in the field of solar energy.

Singapore Polytechnic and Partners to Lead Industry 4.0 Solutions for SMEs to Develop Smart and Sustainable Businesses

  • Developing robust loT systems and bolstering the currency of AloT curriculum: The Mol between SP and Advantech, a global leader in the Internet of Things (loT) intelligent systems and embedded platforms, will help SMEs adopt Artificial Intelligence of Things (AloT) applications and its related technologies to transform their businesses, elevate customer experiences, and accelerate commercial performance.
  • Pivoting the manufacturing industry to utilise advanced technologies and digitalisation: SP and Beckhoff will train individuals to leverage i4.0 technologies and help companies kick-start their i4.0 transformation journeys. The Mol supports Singapore's Manufacturing 2030 vision and aligns with Enterprise Singapore's (ESG) initiatives to pivot the manufacturing industry to 14.0 using smart manufacturing solutions and connectivity standards. By helping SMEs establish niches in the manufacturing value chain, they will be well-placed to capture new growth opportunities, thereby strengthening Singapore's manufacturing competitiveness.
  • Driving Al transformation and accelerating Al knowledge transfer: SP will ink an Mol with SSG, SCCCI and AISG on 19 October 2022 to accelerate the transfer of Al knowledge and Al solution adoption for SMEs in the healthcare, education, cybersecurity, and manufacturing sectors by 2030. The MoU signatories will introduce a new "Best-in-Class in Artificial Intelligence - Al Lead" programme for SMEs to take an Al readiness self- assessment exercise, and gain a deeper understanding of their Al capabilities and business improvement gaps.
